**Green Computing**, which is also known as **sustainable** computing is an designed to be an eco-friendly practice of anything that concerns computers. Designing them, constructing and manufacturing them; even just *using* them. The logic behind green computing is that it's environmentally friendly and energy efficient. Energy efficiency is important because when we talk about computers we're also talking about *servers* which as we know take up a lot of energy. With green computing, the environmental impact is minimzed. Reduces electronic waste and lowers carbon emissions of computing systems. It's a pretty cool idea if you think about it because it sort of combines technology with these sustainaible principles to ensure that our environment doesn't come at the cost of all the technological innovation we've created and have became dependant on.

Now remember when we talked about *servers?*. Well they play a pretty big role when we talk about how technology can affect our environment. Servers and data centers impact the environment through that high energy consumption we mentioned earlier in the first paragraph. In the past **8** years, the total electricity consumption from data centers in the US has went up by rougly 2.1%, which doesn't seem like a difference, but it expected to rise up to 9-12% within the next decade¹.
